People Skills

Dealing with people is one of the hardest but most important skills you can learn. Knowing how to handle a diverse set of people will vastly make your life easier. That starts with knowing your audience. First, you observe the person listen to what they say and watch what they do to get a good sense of the person. Then, put yourself in their shoes; try to see the world as they see it. Finally, prepare and tailor your message for that person. This process will come in handy when dealing with sensitive topics. During difficult discussions, just because someone doesn’t seem like they’ve been swayed doesn’t mean that your message didn’t reach them, some people just like confrontation. Another thing to look out for is that some people won’t like an idea unless they believe they came up with it.

Furthermore, dealing with people has a lot to do with how you carry yourself. If you are comfortable with yourself and believe in your message, you will be a persuasive communicator. You should be respectful to others initially regardless of social status or intelligence. It is unbecoming to be rude to people just because you perceive the person to live life in a fashion you don’t agree with. Remember that everyone is just trying to get through this life the best way they know-how. Additionally, when dealing with people with a higher rank or position than yourself, you should be respectful but, don’t be scared of them and be overly agreeable just because someone has a higher position than you doesn’t make them a better person than you.
